name: pitch-night
description: Make your home market legible to pitch judges who don't know it (the cross-cultural move)
---

Run the ORBWEVA Pitch Night skill (`skills/pitch-night/SKILL.md`), Stage 2.

Take the current pitch and make its local context legible to a judge with zero local knowledge. For each of customer, problem, channel, and competitor:

1. Anchor the unknown to a known reference in five words ("it's the X equivalent of [thing the judge knows]").
2. Convert local units — currency to USD/the judge's currency, local platforms/regulations get one clause of explanation.
3. Show the customer as one named person, not a segment.
4. Name the thing that is true only in this market and turn it into the edge — the local wrinkle a foreign competitor would miss is usually the moat.

If the audience is a specific cross-border pairing (e.g. Australia × Japan, Korea ↔ Japan), spell out the one structural fact that changes the product, and say explicitly why this travels. Reference the full SKILL.md for the technique.
